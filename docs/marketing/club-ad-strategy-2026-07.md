# Reaching clubs on Instagram and TikTok — targeting strategy (2026-07)

The market shape decides the plan: ~1,100-1,600 basketball clubs in Canada,
but only a handful of leagues per province. So paid ads chase **clubs**;
leagues get personal outreach off the census sheet; **players** get the cheap,
viral top of the funnel and pull their parents and clubs in behind them.

## The two-front funnel

**Front 1 — players (TikTok + IG Reels), bottom-up.**
Kids scroll; parents mostly don't. `ad-players` (hook: "Dropped 21 last
night? Prove it.") runs against basketball interest audiences, 13-24, with
the closing card literally instructing the share: "Show your parents."
Optimize for cheap video views and shares, not clicks. Success metric:
3-second hold rate and shares, not conversions. TikTok is THIS front's home;
club decision-makers are not reachable there with paid targeting worth the money.

**Front 2 — clubs (Meta: Instagram + Facebook), top-down.**
Club owners and directors are 30-55 and live on Instagram and Facebook.
`ad-clubs` (hook: "Still chasing e-transfers at midnight?") runs in Ads
Manager with three ad sets:

1. **The census list (our unfair advantage).** We already hold 1,178 clubs
   and 1,023 named contacts (docs/research/canada-master-sheet.xlsx). Upload
   the email list as a Customer List Custom Audience and run `ad-clubs`
   straight at the actual decision-makers. The audience is tiny, so budget is
   small and high frequency is fine — these people should see us weekly.
2. **Lookalike 1-3%** built from that list, geo Canada (start Ontario).
   This finds club-operator-shaped people we never scraped.
3. **Interest stack** for prospecting: interests in TeamSnap, SportsEngine,
   GameChanger, basketball coaching, AAU/rep basketball, age 28-55. People
   Meta knows engage with competitor tools are exactly our buyers.

**Leagues: no ads.** Four or five per province means a spreadsheet, an email,
and a walkthrough link (/for-leagues), not a campaign. The census sheet
already lists them.

## The second touch: "your club already has a page"

The 188+ imported unclaimed Ontario club pages are the retargeting hook.
Once the Meta Pixel is on sportshubone.com, retarget site visitors and
75%-video-viewers with a claim-angle ad: "Your club is already on SportsHub
One. Claim your page." That is a far harder CTA than a cold pitch, and it is
truthful — their page exists today. (Build item: install the Meta Pixel +
ViewContent event on /for-clubs and a claim-start event; needs the owner's
Meta Business account.)

## Creative rotation and testing

- Ship 2-3 hook variants per front and kill weekly on 3s-hold and CPM:
  clubs: "midnight e-transfers" vs "five apps and a spreadsheet" vs "your
  club already has a page"; players: "Prove it." vs "Grandma saw your three."
- All club traffic lands on /for-clubs with UTMs (utm_source=meta,
  utm_campaign=clubs-{hook}); players land on /demo/parents or the app link.
- Starting budget: $20-30/day per ad set. The custom-audience set can run at
  $5-10/day given its size. Expect Meta CPMs roughly $8-15 CAD for this niche.
- Both spots are sound-off legible; add trending audio at upload time on each
  platform (boosts distribution, costs nothing).

## What NOT to do

- No square/landscape video in paid placements: 9:16 for Reels/TikTok/Stories,
  4:5 for feed.
- No line-by-line intro builds: the full hook is on frame 1 (scroll-past rule).
- No paid league campaigns; no TikTok B2B targeting for clubs.

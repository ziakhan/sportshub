Owner brief: name must essentially contain "sports hub" without getting long;
taglines he picked from the earlier round: **"Youth basketball — all of it, one
app"** and **"Your season runs itself"**. Wants homepage marketing that says

## 1. Competitive naming reality (researched 2026-07-18)

Every direct competitor claims "all-in-one" **verbatim**:

| Company | Hero line |
|---|---|
| TeamSnap | "The new standard in club & league management" / "One powerful platform." |
| TeamLinkt | "Sports Registration, Scheduling, and Admin. Handled." |
| SportsEngine | "Made for youth sports. Built for growth and play." |
| GameChanger | "Live the game like never before" |
| Spond | "The app that makes sports happen" |
| LeagueApps | "Everything You Need, All In One Place" |
| Jersey Watch | "The all-in-one platform for youth sports organizations" |
| RAMP | "…all-in-one sports management solution" |

**Conclusion: never say "all-in-one" — the phrase is category wallpaper.
Prove completeness instead (the delete-list + the replaces-map + the
capability checklist).**

Name-collision landscape: SportsHub Games Network / SportsHub Technologies
(Minneapolis, real-money **fantasy** games — different class), 98.5 The Sports
Hub (Boston **radio** — different class), Singapore Sports Hub (venue). Plain
"SportsHub" alone is weakly protectable; a modifier fixes that.

## 2. Name — shortlist + recommendation

Domain status checked via registry whois 2026-07-18:

| Candidate | .com | .ca | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| **SportsHub One** ★ | **sportshubone.com AVAILABLE** | **AVAILABLE** | **RECOMMENDED** |
| Your SportsHub | squatted (NameBright — parked, typ. $1–3k ask) | AVAILABLE | Runner-up |
| One SportsHub | registered (Spaceship, parked) | AVAILABLE | Weaker inversion |
| My SportsHub | squatted (GoDaddy) | AVAILABLE | Generic feel |
| SportsHub HQ | squatted | — | Operator-only vibe |
| plain SportsHub | sportshub.com = fantasy co | taken | Colloquial short form only |

**Recommendation: SportsHub One.**
- The name *is* the positioning — "One" carries one-app/one-account/one-login,
  the exact thesis of both taglines.
- Exact .com **and** .ca hand-registerable today (~$35 total) — no squatter
  negotiation, register immediately even if undecided.
- Everyday speech naturally shortens to "SportsHub" (what the owner wants
  people to say) while the registered mark stays distinctive.
- Product-line headroom: SportsHub **Plus** (we already gate features on
  Plus), SportsHub **Live**, per-sport verticals under one house brand.
- Not youth-locked, not basketball-locked — survives men's leagues +
  volleyball.

Runner-up **Your SportsHub** retcons the existing domain (ysportshub.com →
"Y = Your"), pairs beautifully with "Your season runs itself", and .ca is
free — but reads consumer-warm and weaker on the operator/B2B side, and the
.com needs a squatter buyout.

Either way: keep ysportshub.com as a permanent redirect.

## 3. Tagline system — both winners, two different jobs

- **Master brand tagline (evergreen, sport-agnostic):**
  **"Your season runs itself."**
  Under the logo, app-store subtitle, email footer, business cards.
- **Category headline formula (the hero H1, per vertical):**
  **"[Youth basketball]. All of it. One app."**
  Today: "Youth basketball. All of it. One app."
  Later: "Youth volleyball. All of it. One app." / "Your league. All of it.
  One app." (men's rec sales page). The formula scales; the master tagline
  never changes.
- They work as a pair: the formula is the claim, "Your season runs itself" is
  the payoff line that closes the paragraph.

Support lines (system, reusable):
- Eyebrow: **"The first complete platform for youth basketball"** (claim
  discipline in §6 — keep the qualifier while basketball is the wedge).
- Delete-list: **"No spreadsheets. No email chains. No group-chat chaos.
  No 'one more app.'"**
- Pile line: "They each do a piece. Nobody does the whole thing."

**SECTION — THE PILE (delete the patchwork)**
- Eyebrow: SOUND FAMILIAR?

**SECTION — THE REPLACES-MAP (names competitors, nominative + factual)**

  - Team comms & RSVPs → *replaces TeamSnap, Spond, the WhatsApp group*

## 6. Claims discipline (legal/accuracy guardrails)

- **"First complete platform for youth basketball"** — keep the *basketball*
  qualifier. Defensible: no competitor ships club ops + league ops +
  basketball-native live scoring/stats + auto recaps + payments + comms in
  one app (TeamLinkt is closest and runs standings-only in its basketball
  deployments; GameChanger has no ops; TeamSnap has no stat depth). Do NOT
  claim "first" for generic youth *sports* — Jersey Watch/LeagueApps/RAMP
  all claim all-in-one there.
- Competitor naming = nominative use: text only, no logos, factual "does a
  piece" framing, no pricing or quality disparagement.
- No pricing claims anywhere (business model not final), no "free", no user
  counts we can't source.
- Never say "all-in-one" (§1).

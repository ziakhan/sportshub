## Operator

**Owned and operated by Ontario Basketball (OBA)** — the provincial sport org under
Canada Basketball. Launched May 2015 (originally with Pretty River Sports &
Entertainment); now runs inside OBA's High Performance department.

**Joining:** application/business case to OBA High Performance (historically an RFP:
letter of intent → application package by email; ongoing applications accepted).
All programs + athletes need OBA membership; athletes register annually via RAMP.
**Fees not published** — confirm via jdawkins@ / info@basketball.on.ca.

**Season 2025-26:** OSBA Men's (14 teams), OSBA Women's (16), Trillium Men's (27),
D-League Boys (20), D-League Girls (17) — 38 distinct orgs. Fall Showcase Sep 26–28
2025 (Bill Crothers SS); Championships Mar 5–8 2026 (Centennial College + Toronto
Pan Am). Finals: Ridley vs FEIA (M), Royal Crown 56–55 Crestwood (W), Father Henry
Carr 79–73 Tall Pines (Trillium M). FIBA rules, 24s shot clock.

**Stack (competitive intel):** whole league on **RAMP InterActive** (site,
schedules, standings, rosters, leaders, registration); video/stats via **Synergy
Sports**; streaming **OSBA TV (YouTube)** + **SportsCanada.TV** via **Pixellot**
auto-broadcast. The best-tooled league in the province — displacement is hard;
content/player-page angles are the wedge.

## Confidence & gaps

- **High:** division lists from the league's own live site (© 2026; championship
  results Mar 5–8 2026). Operator staff/contacts from OBA's own directory.
- **Second-best:** 7 programs have no working site — official IG/X handles given.
  Crestwood's info@ appears only on third-party directories and was excluded.
- **Gaps:** membership fees unpublished; "Trillium Women's"/"Trillium+" divisions
  have pages but no 2025-26 team lists; ontariosba.ca blocks non-browser fetchers.
- **List hygiene:** Hodan Prep folding after 2025-26; Southwest Academy dropped
  from OSBA Men's to D-League Girls.

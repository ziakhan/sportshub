# Canada Youth Basketball Landscape 2026 — All Provinces ex-Ontario

> Companion to [[gta-league-landscape-2026]] and `ontario-basketball-clubs.csv` (Ontario, done 2026-07).
> Club-level census lives in **`canada-basketball-clubs.csv`** (this folder) — 964 clubs, 17 columns
> incl. owner/principal, per-league team mapping, software, confidence, source URL.
> Method: 2 deep-research workflow runs, 64 agents (league map → city-by-city census → adversarial
> verify + contact enrichment), ~7.2M tokens, 2026-07-14. Confidence labels per entry:
> **verified** = 2+ independent sources · **primary** = official site · **reported** = single secondary · **uncertain**.

## Executive summary

- **Coverage: 141 leagues + 10 governing bodies + 964 clubs** across QC (149 clubs), BC (229), AB (203),
  MB (95), SK (107), NS (65), NB (53), NL (33), PEI (13), territories (17). Cities censused largest→smallest
  per province, with PSO member registries swept for small towns. Bound of the method: orgs with zero web
  presence don't surface; counts are "discoverable clubs", not population truth.
- **Verification: 112 adversarial spot-checks — 105 confirmed, 4 plausible, 3 refuted** (corrections applied
  in CSV + tables; see per-province "Verification spot-checks"). NB, NL and PEI got **no verification pass**
  (session limits) — treat those sections as primary-source-only.
- **Software landscape (the competitive map):** **RAMP dominates Canada** — 45 of 141 leagues and 117 of 964
  clubs run on it (AB/MB/NS almost wholesale). **TeamLinkt is the fast #2** (16 leagues / 69 clubs, near-total
  in Saskatchewan) — consistent with [[competitor-tracker]] calling it the top Canadian threat. Legacy layer
  ripe for replacement: GOALLINE (8/15), eSportsDesk, itSportsNet, LeagueToolbox remnants. Quebec is its own
  stack: Spordle/Publication Sports + **Kreezee** for PSO leagues, Amilia/Zeffy for club payments. US platforms
  (SportsEngine, TeamSnap, LeagueApps, Exposure) hold niches, mostly BC/academies. Nobody owns game-day.
- **Expansion-strategy targets confirmed by census** ([[expansion-strategy-2026-07]] holds up): Halifax **MBA
  350+ teams** (RAMP, single operator) · Edmonton **EYBA 2,500+ players/~150 teams** (all-RAMP) · Winnipeg
  **WMBA 200+ teams/6 leagues** (RAMP) · **BCCBA 24→196 teams** in 4 seasons (no real platform) · Calgary
  **CMBA 4,500 players/10 zones** (RAMP, federated sale).
- **New targets this census surfaced:** **V&DABA Victoria — 188 teams, 1,800+ players, 92 games/week** (community
  nonprofit, thin stack) · **TCYBA Tri-Cities 2,000+ players** (TeamSnap) · Gatineau **LMBG/ABG ~500 players** ·
  **LSIBL** Saskatchewan Indigenous league (TeamLinkt, Canada Basketball unified partner) · **JANT** BC First
  Nations championships (129 teams/1,856 athletes 2026) · NB's tournament-circuit model (Google Forms + RAMP CMS).
- **Quebec is structurally different, as expected:** competitive U14–U18 basketball is school-based (**RSEQ**,
  out of scope as varsity); the civil club layer = PSO's CBQ/LBQ + independent **Montreal Basketball League**
  (~20 member clubs, Spordle). French-first product remains the gate ([[expansion-strategy-2026-07]] §Quebec).
- **Contact/ownership yield:** ~86% of clubs have direct contact info; 546 have a named owner/president/director.
  Weakest: BC (~130 clubs missing direct contact — many community associations run via city rec portals), NB.

- **DBC Youth Basketball League (YBL)** — Self-described as one of the most affordable youth leagues in Montreal; DBC also runs a youth fall-winter season, summer camps and adult programs. Dexter's Basketball Club appears in Basketball Québec's Montréal and Rive-Sud regional club registries.

- **Miller Basketball Academy leagues (Laval)** — Private-academy in-house league model (pay-to-play via e-commerce checkout rather than a league-management platform). Scale and season windows not published; single-source.
- **Ligues de matchs EMBQ (École de mini-basketball de Québec)** — Listed in Basketball Québec's Québec/Chaudière-Appalaches regional registry ('L'École de Mini-Basketball de Québec', Lac-Beauport). League notes:
- **CMBA Fall/Winter League (Calgary Minor Basketball Association)** — CMBA self-describes as 'Where youth basketball begins'. Runs Sizzling Summer Camps (U9-U15) and Holiday Hoopla winter mini-camp. Zone associations (e.g. Airdrie airdriebasketball.ca) are RAMP sites.
- **CMBA Spring League (club season)** — This is THE Calgary club spring league — CMBA takes club-team registrations only in spring. Zones lend uniforms and buy AYBC insurance (via CMBA) so spring zone teams can rent gyms and enter outside tournaments (documented on Airdrie AMBA spring page).
- **CMBA Diamond Prep League (fall HS girls)** — $400 + $20 non-refundable tryout fee. Fills the fall gap before school HS season for senior girls.
- **EYBA Fall/Winter League (Edmonton Youth Basketball Association)** — EYBA owns its own facility (The EYBA Centre). Adult arm (EBA, edmontonbasketball.org, TeamLinkt) excluded as adult-only. Constituent associations run their own RAMP sites (e.g. southwestbasketball.com, sbahoops.ca).
- **EYBA Spring League** — Open/Club fee $1,850/team incl. referees (EBOA-licensed officials). Venues: EYBA Centre, Saville Community Sports Centre, Legacy Athletics. This is the main Edmonton club spring league (Calgary equivalent = CMBA Spring).
- **Edmonton Youth Premier League (EYPL)** — $525 incl. $50 tryout fee + jersey/gear. Scheduled on Sundays specifically to avoid conflict with EYBA Saturday league play. Girls at EYBA Centre, boys at Legacy Athletics.
- **Alberta Basketball Youth Provincials (YP)** — Entry fees 2026: $575 for 5v5 divisions, $275 for 18U girls 3x3. The championship tournament for community youth basketball in Alberta — effectively the season-capper for CMBA/EYBA winter leagues.
- **Alberta Basketball Club Championship (ABA Club Invitational)** — The provincial season-ending event for AYBC/spring club teams. tourny.ca usage alongside RAMP is competitive intel — ABA uses a separate tournament-bracket SaaS for event ops.
- **Alberta Basketball 3X3 (series)** — Portal exists in ABA Games Portal league menu but site content is still RAMP demo articles — program appears early-stage/event-based rather than a scheduled league.
- **Alberta Basketball 4 Directions Invitational** — Listed as one of six leagues/competitions in the ABA Games Portal menu; serves as an identification event toward North American Indigenous Games 2027.
- **AYBC — Association of Alberta Youth Basketball Clubs (spring/summer club circuit)** — The connective tissue of Alberta club/spring basketball: sells the participation insurance that lets spring club teams rent gyms, play exhibitions and enter tournaments outside CMBA/EYBA league play. Brief called it 'Alberta Youth Basketball Conference ~50 clubs' — correct name is Association of Alberta Youth Basketball Clubs; the 50-club figure matches its published counter.
- **Red Deer Minor Basketball Association (RDMBA) leagues** — One of only three direct 'association' members on ABA's registry (with EYBA and LMBA). SportsEngine stack is an outlier in RAMP-dominated Alberta. Central Alberta Basketball Club (cabcsky.com, ~10 teams) is the area's rep club.
- **Lethbridge Minor Basketball Association (LMBA) leagues** — Southern-Alberta club scene around it: Lethbridge Blazers, Coaldale Basketball Club, Cardston Youth Basketball, Magrath Rockets (ABA/AYBC members) — small-town teams enter Youth Provincials directly.
- **Med Hat Minor Basketball League** — $380 incl. keeper game jersey; KidSport/Jumpstart eligible; sells out annually per club site. A club-run league — Medicine Hat has no separate minor basketball association; Badlands Basketball (AYBC member) is the local rep club. Site could not be crawled directly (DNS), details from indexed official pages.

- **St. Albert Youth Basketball League (SAYBL)** — Runs parallel to St. Albert SLAM (the competitive EYBA association) — SAYBL is the town's rec alternative. Uses school-board + city gyms.
- **3x3 Edmonton Youth League** — Small independent format league — evidence that private facility operators (Legacy Athletics also hosts EYPL and EYBA spring games) are spawning their own leagues.

League notes:
- **Saskatoon Minor Basketball Association (SMBA)** — Largest youth basketball operation in Saskatchewan's largest city; the biggest single TeamLinkt displacement target in the province. Community-association feeder structure is unusual (teams built by neighbourhood associations, SMBA schedules centrally).
- **Regina Community Basketball Association (RCBA)** — Regina's main recreational/house youth league; complement to the club-only RYCBL.
- **Regina Youth Club Basketball League (RYCBL)** — This is the game-circuit for Regina rep clubs (Ballers, Regina Raptors, 306 Heat etc.) between tournaments — the closest SK analogue to a GTA club league. Website is a 'temporary page until our website is complete' — potentially receptive to a better platform.
- **Living Skies Indigenous Basketball League (LSIBL)** — Saskatchewan's first province-wide Indigenous youth league; grant-funded, cost-free model; BSI member. Distinct multi-community logistics (hub communities, cultural workshops alongside games).
- **HoopLife League (Regina, fall/winter)** — Private-academy league model (premium price point ~3x community leagues); BSI member. One of only two Citrus Camps users found in SK.
- **HoopLife Spring League (Regina)** — Also runs an adult 3x3 rec league (16+) year-round — out of scope but shows facility utilization model.
- **Moose Jaw Minor Basketball League (Golden Ticket Sports)** — Case study: volunteer association collapsed (2023) and a private facility operator absorbed the league. Old domain mjmba.com now appears dead/hijacked. Only LeagueApps footprint found in SK youth basketball.
- **Prince Albert Community Basketball Association (PACBA) Winter League** — Only RAMP-based youth basketball league found in SK (RAMP otherwise appears in SK for other sports and the PSO's own registrations).
- **SKY Basketball / Saskatchewan Youth Basketball (Jr. NBA)** — The PSO's own grassroots league for small-town SK. Community list/session dates on the TeamLinkt Communities page include stale 2020 references — per-area community counts should be re-verified at registration time.
- **BSI Club Championships (Basketball Saskatchewan Provincials)** — The anchor event of the SK rep-club calendar; the reason clubs affiliate with BSI.
- **BSI-sanctioned spring club tournament circuit (2026)** — SK has no year-round provincial club league — rep clubs practice all winter (many players also in SMBA/RCBA/RYCBL) then compete via this May-June tournament circuit + provincials. Aggregate entry; individual tournaments are weekend events, not standing leagues.
- **Saskatchewan Summer Games — Basketball** — Not a club league — district all-star competition — included for landscape completeness since it drives spring ID camps and district team programs.

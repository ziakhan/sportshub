## Pricing model — recommendation (v1, for owner discussion)

Three axes, mapped to the census:

1. **League operators — per-team-per-season** ($15–$30/team/season, volume
   tiers). They already denominate in team entries. 824 visible entries ≈
   $12k–25k/yr from the enumerated circuits alone; OBL at ~1,200 teams is the
   prize. Includes scoring, standings, scoresheets, recaps, league pages.
   First season free for the lighthouse league.
2. **Clubs — team-count bands** (from the actual distribution):
   - **Starter (1–2 teams · 59% of clubs): free.** Claim page, rosters,
     schedule/stat aggregation. This is the funnel + the network effect.
   - **Growth (3–9 teams · ~35%): ~$79–99/mo.** Registrations, tryouts,
     offers, payments, branded pages, multi-league identity.
   - **Pro (10+ teams · ~6%, incl. every big rep club): ~$199–249/mo.**
     White-label, staff roles at scale, priority support.
3. **Families — Family Pass** (~$10/mo, planned P3) + payment processing
   margin on registrations (the largest long-term pool: OBA's ~16,000 athletes
   × typical $500–800 club fees ≈ $8–13M/yr flowing through Ontario rep
   registrations; a 1–2% processing margin is $80–260k/yr at full capture).

Segment nuances: scholastic preps (1–6 teams but tuition-level budgets) fit
Growth by count but will pay Pro for recruitment/exposure features (player
pages, film, streaming integrations) — consider an **Exposure add-on** priced
per program rather than per team. House leagues: free registration tier,
monetize payments only.

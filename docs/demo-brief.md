# DEMO PAGES BRIEF (owner's instructions, verbatim consolidation, 2026-07-19)

## The goal
From the homepage there are buttons for clubs, for leagues, and for parents
and players. Each leads to a page where that audience sees the capabilities
of the system, each and every one of them, as realistically as possible.
Clubs see everything they can run. Leagues see the power they have from
this platform: scoring, content, recaps, scheduling, assigning referees,
live real-time push-based scoring, not refreshing the page. Parents see all
the advantages, and not just the administrative part: for parents the
content matters too (scores, news, recaps, following their kid).

## The medium, non-negotiable
Screens are built in a clean design system, slightly zoomed and more
readable than raw screenshots, but they must reflect the real product
screens as close as possible, never far from the truth. The rule that makes
this work: before drawing any screen, open the actual product screen for
that step and take the real fields, real labels, real steps, and real rules
from it. Simplifying means leaving chrome out, never inventing anything
that is not in the product. If a screen cannot be checked against the real
product, it does not get built. No screen recordings (video tutorials come
later). Realistic data on every screen: full rosters with many players, not
two names and a "plus 10 more."

## The framing
Admin and operator actions are shown on a PC-type screen, almost full
screen (minus maybe the top menus), showing a lot, not a zoomed-in
fragment. Parent and family views are shown inside a real iPhone frame that
actually looks like a phone, full screen of the phone, correct proportions,
not distorted or half-size. Everything must display well on mobile: no
zooming out to read, dimensions matter.

## The presentation
The screens transition through the flow in order, each with a description
of one or two sentences saying what is happening. The user goes at their
own pace: reading a screen for as long as they want, and clicking the
highlighted button moves to the next screen, like using the real app. The
transitions matter: button presses should feel real, with confirmations
appearing (green ticks, "offer sent"). Instructions on screen must be
crystal clear so nobody can make a mistake (for example: "Go through the
steps at your own pace, click the highlighted button to continue"). It can
be broken into chapters, but the end-to-end flow has to go in order,
switching personas as the real season does.

## The flow, in order, from signing up for trials to trophies

League setup first:
1. League creates a season
2. Adds sessions and session dates
3. Adds venues
4. Referees
5. Sets fees, roster lock date, and scheduling rules (minimum and maximum
   games per week, per session)
6. Finalizes and publishes the league, registration opens

Club setup:
7. Club signs up, or claims its existing club page
8. Creates teams
9. Assigns staff: head coach, assistant coach, team manager, and inviting a
   coach by email

Tryouts through rosters:
10. Club creates a tryout: dates, venue and gym, capacity, how many sessions
11. Posts the tryout
12. Parents discover the tryout
13. Get the details
14. Sign up: registration and payment
15. Attendance at the tryout dates
16. Club sees signup status
17. Offer templates: uniforms or no uniforms, installments, season start
    and end, practice dates to come later
18. Send offers
19. Parent accepts the offer: selecting the template fields the team sent,
    jersey sizes and selection, payment plan
20. It submits back to the club
21. Finalizing the roster, finalizing the team

League entry:
22. Club presses one button, looks at its rosters, selects the roster to
    submit for this league, and sends it, payment included
23. Roster locks and cannot be changed after the deadline date
24. League reviews the payments paid by the teams
25. League finalizes on the deadline
26. One button generates the entire schedule, or session by session, within
    the configured rules

The season, to the trophies:
27. Live scoring, shown side by side: one screen adds a score, and the live
    public game page updates in real time next to it. Side by side means
    visible together, including on a phone
28. Scores, box scores, standings
29. News and recaps generated after games
30. Chats and polls
31. Playoffs through to the championship

## Coverage bar
That is 20-plus scenarios and most need multiple screens: registration
forms, payment screens, confirmation screens, both sides of the offer.
Expect 50 or more screens total. A handful of screens does not cover the
system, and nothing partial gets reviewed: every scenario above must have
its screens before showing the owner.

## Quality rules
All text reads like a human wrote it: no em-dashes, nothing that sounds
like AI. Age groups are a single calendar year (born 2013, never "2013 or
2014"), because that is how it works in Canada. Every screen must be
checked against the real product screen it represents before it counts as
done. Before showing anything: every page must load on the live site, every
screen must be reviewed by eye, no 404s anywhere, and nothing on any screen
that the real product does not actually do.

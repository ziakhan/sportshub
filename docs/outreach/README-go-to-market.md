# Go-to-market — strategy & outreach kit (2026-07-06)

## The plan in one line

**Land your two friends' leagues first (NPH + NJC/NSC), use them as living
proof, then let the leagues pull their clubs in — while a low-touch social +
"claim your page" motion works the club long tail in parallel.**

## Why this order (updated for the warm intros)

Before, "leagues vs clubs first" was a judgment call. It isn't anymore — you
have warm relationships with the operators of **NPH** (the biggest multi-league
youth operator in Canada) and the **NJC/NSC** circuits. That collapses the two
hardest problems in B2B sales — getting the meeting and being trusted — for the
two highest-leverage accounts in the whole market. So:

1. **Lighthouse (now):** one of the two friends runs a season on SportsHub.
   NJC/NSC is the ideal *first* pilot — small (~80 teams), centralized at ONE
   venue (Six Park East, Oshawa), currently on generic TeamLinkt. Live scoring
   at one gym on weekend sessions is a clean, contained proof. NPH is the bigger
   prize but also a bigger lift (multiple leagues, a home-grown stats site they're
   attached to) — approach in parallel but expect a longer runway.
2. **Leagues pull clubs (next):** every club in a piloted league gets a
   pre-built, claimable page — their teams, schedule, and stats already live.
   "Claim your club" becomes the acquisition funnel; registration/tryouts/
   payments monetize them after.
3. **Long-tail club motion (parallel, low-touch):** social demos + ads + the
   "claim your page" email to the ~205 researched clubs. Cheap, always-on,
   compounds the network.

## Sequencing the two friends

- **NJC/NSC (Tony House, Ottawa) — first pilot.** Smallest decision, one venue,
  weakest incumbent tooling, senior-prep audience that lives for exposure. Pitch:
  live scoring + auto stats/standings + player pages + AI recaps, replacing the
  TeamLinkt patchwork. Offer the pilot season free.
- **NPH (Tariq & Elias Sbiet, Burlington) — strategic partner.** They already
  built a stats platform, so don't pitch "get organized" — pitch what their
  stack can't do: club-branded pages, a family-facing live experience, AI recaps
  as a content engine (they're a media company — this multiplies their output),
  player profiles across all their leagues, and eventually a paid syndication hub.
  Frame it as powering NPA + Showcase + D1 on one system.

## The three things to settle before mass CLUB outreach

1. **OB-002 — claim-club trust model.** When we email/adv clubs, they hit "Claim
   your club." That flow's trust questions (stale info@ inboxes, who approves,
   SLA) must be resolved first. It's the landing experience for everything.
2. **Contact enrichment** — the ~110 clubs with no email are being researched
   now; merge results into the clubs sheet before any club send.
3. **CASL compliance** — every outreach email must identify us (name, mailing
   address), be relevant B2B, and carry an unsubscribe line. Templates here
   already include it.

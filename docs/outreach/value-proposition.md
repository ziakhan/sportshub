# Value proposition — benefits by audience

Grounded in what's actually shipped (live scoring, AI recaps, standings, player
pages, payments) and the researched pains (NPH's home-grown stats site, NJC on
generic TeamLinkt, OBL on Google Sheets, clubs fragmented across 3-4 systems).

## The one sentence

**SportsHub turns a youth basketball league into a live, ESPN-style experience
— games scored from a phone courtside, standings and stats that update
themselves, auto-written recaps, and a page for every club, team, and player —
so families stay glued and operators stop living in spreadsheets.**

---

## For LEAGUE OPERATORS (NPH, NJC/NSC, OBL, Coalition…)

**The pain today:** schedules in Google Sheets or a generic US tournament app;
standings updated by hand; stats scattered or nonexistent; a stats site someone
had to build and maintain; nothing families actually want to open.

**What they get:**
- **Live scoring from any phone** at the scorer's table — no hardware. Scores go
  live instantly for every family watching from home or the next gym.
- **Standings, stats & leaders that compute themselves** from the same event
  stream — no manual entry, no stale "2021-22" pages.
- **Official scoresheets** — server-generated PDF, referee sign-off with
  signature + PIN, emailed to both clubs on finalize. Replaces the paper binder.
- **AI game recaps auto-published** the second a game ends — a headline, a story,
  a cover image, top performers. A content engine that runs itself.
- **A public league hub** — scores, standings, news, schedules, and a page for
  every team and player. Your league looks like theScore, not a PDF.
- **Player profiles with season stats** — the recruitment-exposure layer prep
  and elite programs pay attention to.
- **One system** for schedule → scoring → stats → registration → payments.

**For NPH specifically:** you already have a stats site — this is the layer it
can't be: club-branded pages, a family-facing live product, recaps that multiply
your media output across NPA + Showcase + D1 at once, and a path to a paid
syndication hub. It makes your whole ecosystem feel like one modern network.

**For NJC/NSC specifically:** replace the TeamLinkt patchwork with something
built for basketball — live scoring at Six Park, auto standings across your
weekend sessions, and player pages your senior-prep athletes will actually share
with recruiters. One venue, one clean pilot.

---

## For CLUBS (rep, prep, hybrid)

**The pain today:** the same club plays NPH + NJC + OSBA + a rep league, and its
life is scattered across four different apps, none of which talk to each other.
Registration in one place, schedules in another, stats nowhere.

**What they get:**
- **One club identity across every league you play in** — all your teams,
  schedules, and results in one branded place, no matter whose circuit the game
  was in. Nobody else offers this.
- **A free, claimable club page** — your teams and season already there; claim
  it and it's yours.
- **Registration, tryouts, offers & online payments** — families sign up and pay
  in a few taps; money settles to you instantly (Stripe).
- **Every player's stat history** follows them across seasons and leagues.
- **Parents get live scores, box scores, highlights, and recaps** for your teams
  — the engagement that keeps families renewing.

---

## For FAMILIES (parents & players)

**The pain today:** you drive to a gym, or refresh a dead Google Sheet, to find
out if your kid's team won. Highlights live in a coach's phone. Stats don't exist.

**What they get:**
- **Follow your kid across every team and league** from one home screen.
- **Live scores & box scores** as the game happens.
- **Highlights, photos, and an auto-written recap** after every game.
- **Season stats and a real player page** — a keepsake and a recruiting asset.
- **Family Pass** (coming) — premium highlights/stats/keepsakes; the record is
  free, the relationship is premium.

---

## Why now / why us (the wedge)

- **Live scoring + auto content is the hook** no incumbent here has — TeamLinkt,
  Exposure, RAMP, LeagueApps are registration/schedule tools, not live
  experiences with recaps and player pages.
- **We already run on the real Ontario map** — 300+ clubs imported, a live
  showcase league with scored games and recaps to demo, not vaporware.
- **One platform, three sides** — leagues, clubs, and families on the same graph,
  which is exactly why the fragmentation stops here.
